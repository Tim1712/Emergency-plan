Immediate Diver Response

First stabilize the diver, then collect information.

2

Start AB-CABs if required

3

Give oxygen early

4

Call EMS / DAN / chamber support

Case 1

Unresponsive Diver

No response, abnormal breathing, collapse, or suspected cardiac emergency.

Symptoms / Situation

  • Not responding
  • May not be breathing normally
  • Possible collapse after dive
  • Possible cardiac arrest
↓

Immediate Action

  • Maintain AB-CABs
  • Provide rescue breathing if needed
  • Start CPR if necessary
  • Activate EMS immediately
  • Prepare for evacuation
Case 2

Responsive Diver

Awake but showing possible dive-related symptoms.

Symptoms / Situation

  • Awake and responsive
  • Pain, dizziness, fatigue, or breathing trouble
  • Possible DCI / barotrauma signs
↓

Immediate Action

  • Encourage diver to lie down and relax
  • Give 100% oxygen
  • Protect from excess heat or cold
  • If oxygen is limited, give the highest concentration possible as long as it lasts
  • If oxygen is empty but enriched air nitrox is available, you may give that

Decompression Illness (DCI)

Treat suspicious symptoms seriously. Give oxygen early, keep the diver at rest, and collect clear information for medical professionals.

Suspected DCI?
Give oxygen → monitor continuously → call EMS / DAN / chamber support.

Possible Symptoms

  • Pain in joints
  • Fatigue
  • Extreme fatigue
  • Dizziness
  • Paralysis
  • Unconsciousness
  • Cardiac arrest
  • Nausea
  • Blurred vision
  • Vertigo
  • Loss of sensation
  • Coughing

Ask The Diver

  • Diving today / compressed air?
  • Forced or rapid ascent?
  • Maximum depth?
  • Bottom time?
  • Excessively tired?
  • Where does it hurt?
  • Dizzy?
  • Any numbness or tingling?
  • Trouble breathing?
Oxygen Early Give oxygen as soon as DCI is suspected.
Keep Still Lie the diver down and reduce unnecessary movement.
Escalate Fast Call EMS, DAN, and chamber support when needed.

Heat Emergency

Heat Stroke

Serious, life-threatening overheating. Treat immediately.

Symptoms

  • Hot, dry, flushed skin
  • Rapid pulse
  • Disoriented, confused, or unconscious
  • Life-threatening
→

What To Do

  • Perform a primary assessment
  • Monitor patient using the Cycle of Care
  • Move patient to a cool, shady area
  • Immediately cool by spraying or sponging with cool water
  • Cover with wet clothes and monitor until EMS arrives
  • Replace wet clothes with dry ones when temperature returns to normal
Heat Emergency

Heat Exhaustion

Cooling and rehydration are key. Watch for deterioration.

Symptoms

  • Cool and clammy skin
  • Weak pulse
  • Nausea
  • Dizziness
  • Weakness and anxiety
→

What To Do

  • Move patient to a cool location
  • Lay patient down and elevate legs
  • Provide cool water or electrolyte drink every few minutes
  • Cool by misting with water and fanning
  • Continue support until fully cooled
  • If not calling EMS, encourage patient to see a doctor
  • If condition deteriorates, place in recovery position and activate EMS

Minor Injuries

Minor Injury

Cuts

Symptoms

  • Open wound
  • Bleeding
  • Dirt or particles in wound
↓

What To Do

  • Control bleeding with direct pressure
  • Wash wound with water
  • Cover with non-adhesive dressing and bandage securely
  • Check daily for signs of infection
Minor Injury

Bruises

Symptoms

  • Painful impact area
  • Swelling / discoloration
  • Tenderness
↓

What To Do

  • Apply cold compress as soon as possible
  • Elevate the affected area if possible
Minor Injury

Stings

Symptoms

  • Pain / burning
  • Visible sting or affected skin area
  • Localized irritation
↓

What To Do

  • Remove stings
  • Treat with hot water immersion when possible

Missing Diver Procedure

  1. Have someone call for emergency help while you find out where anyone last saw the missing diver.
  2. Assign spotters to look for bubbles and direct rescuers to the area.
  3. Try to determine if the missing diver may have left without telling anyone.
  4. Look for the diver’s clothes, gear bag, and other identifiable items.
  5. Immediately assign qualified divers to head to the area where the diver was last seen and begin an underwater search.
  6. Assign two skin divers to mark the search area with buoys.
